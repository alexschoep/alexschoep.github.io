Aerial Lighting and Imaging System

I was part of a team that designed and built an embedded system to improve lighting conditions during drone photography. Our goal was to create a studio lighting system that could be mounted to a commercial quadcopter. The full documentation can be downloaded by clicking the following link.

github.com/alexschoep/Aerial-Lighting-and-Imaging

We also made a video to describe the design of our system and demonstrate our prototype.


We tested our prototype on a DJI Phantom quadcopter.



We constructed the the circuit on a breadboard and mounted it to the rear side of the drone. The LED arrays were put at the front. All of the parts were mounted to the legs of the drone.


Our system involves two separate units. We referred to them as the LED Unit and the Remote Unit. The LED Unit is mounted to the drone. The Remote Unit is kept with the user so that they can control the operation of the system. The two units transmit and receive data over radio. Below is a diagram of our system.


The device has automatic and manual adjustment modes. In manual mode, the brightness of the LED's are controlled directly by the user with a potentiometer on the Remote Unit. In automatic mode, the brightness is adjusted based on data from a photoresistor on the LED Unit. All information about the system is displayed on an LCD connected to the Remote Unit.
